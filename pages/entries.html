Everything Else

Caption: "The brain sculpture resembles the brain and the complexity of its inherent brain activity patterns. From a global point of view, a from moment-to-moment varying set of diverse regions is simultaneously active, partly engaging in different processes. Researchers aim to disentangle these processes to better understand their relationships which ultimately is assumed to provide insights about the functioning of the brain. The diversity of processes and regions is reflected by the different forms and colours of lights. Entangled wires demonstrate the complexity of connectivity patterns in the brain. (I made this all up after finishing the brain sculpture. My main aim was to put colourful lights into a brain.)" Caption: "Don't embarrass me in front of my friends, mom!!!" Caption: "“This is a freehand machine embroidery piece which I created in 2016 from two participant scans*. I traced the T1 scans (cut on the middle saggtital slice before normalisation) onto dissolvable stabiliser, then used my Grandmother's sewing machine to fill in the structure and stitch the design onto an old bedsheet. It is a really fun technique to use - Kirstie Allsopp once described this technique as “the crack-cocaine of crafting” - as you attempt to marry the speed of the machine with your attention to the design details, control of the fabric and placement of the stitches. The cerebellum was particularly satisfying. I arranged the scans in such a way as to remind us of the humanity behind our data, with the erratic energy of the stitch evoking the electrical activity of the brain."
